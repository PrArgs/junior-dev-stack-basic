What is Slack?

Slack describes itself as the “operating system for work” — a conversational interface that brings together people, processes and data【636834211350128†L120-L151】. Work happens in channels, which are dedicated spaces that bring the right people together【636834211350128†L130-L136】. With more than 2,600 apps in the Slack marketplace, you can integrate tools such as GitHub, Jira and Google Drive【636834211350128†L141-L145】.

Types of conversations

  • Direct messages (DMs) – Use DMs for conversations between two people (up to eight). Keep them to small groups【598078870387260†L17-L27】.
  • Public channels – Most team discussions should take place in public channels so that decisions and context are visible to everyone【598078870387260†L30-L43】.
  • Private channels – Use private channels only for sensitive or confidential topics; only members of the channel can see the conversation【598078870387260†L45-L52】.
  • Team/important channels – Teams often have dedicated channels where announcements must be acknowledged with an emoji【598078870387260†L58-L63】.
  • Watercooler and feed channels – Use these for casual discussion and automated messages respectively【598078870387260†L64-L70】.

Good practices

  • Label your channels – Use a predictable naming convention such as brand–category–purpose so team members can find information easily【598078870387260†L71-L78】.
  • Keep your profile updated – Ensure your Slack profile (name, avatar and time zone) is accurate so colleagues know who you are and when it’s appropriate to contact you【598078870387260†L82-L97】.
  • Move complex discussions to video – If a discussion requires more than a few back‑and‑forth messages, hop on a quick call and document decisions in the channel later【598078870387260†L97-L107】.
  • Write in paragraphs – Avoid sending many short messages; instead, write complete thoughts in a single message to reduce notification noise【598078870387260†L110-L120】.
  • Label your conversations and use threads – Start messages with a bold summary and reply in threads to keep conversations organized【598078870387260†L126-L144】.
  • Use checkmarks to acknowledge – When a message doesn’t require a response, react with a ✅ to indicate you’ve read it【598078870387260†L140-L152】.
  • Manage your Slack time – Set expectations for response times and block dedicated times to check Slack so you’re not distracted all day【598078870387260†L154-L167】.

Getting started with Slack

  1. Join or create a workspace – If your company already uses Slack, join the workspace. Otherwise, create a new workspace and invite your team.
  2. Complete your profile – Add a profile picture, name and time zone【598078870387260†L82-L97】.
  3. Explore channels – Join the default channels (e.g., #general) and create new channels for specific projects or teams. Use public channels whenever possible【598078870387260†L30-L43】.
  4. Send messages and threads – Start messages with a clear subject and use threads to keep discussions organized【598078870387260†L126-L144】.
  5. Integrate tools – Slack’s app directory includes thousands of integrations. Connect Jira and GitHub to receive notifications and automate workflows.
