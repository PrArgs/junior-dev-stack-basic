What is Jira?

Jira is a project‑management platform from Atlassian that helps teams plan, organize and track their work. It can be used by any team—from software engineers to product managers or marketers—and its workflows and boards can be customized to fit your process【765263728887536†L224-L229】.

Getting started: seven steps

Atlassian’s beginner guide recommends the following steps to set up your first Jira project:

  1. Create a space – Log into your Jira site and select Spaces → Create space to start a new project【765263728887536†L242-L246】.
  2. Pick a template – Jira offers Scrum, Kanban and bug‑tracking templates. Choose the one that matches your team’s workflow【765263728887536†L247-L276】.
  3. Set up your columns – Configure the board’s columns to reflect your workflow. In team‑managed spaces you can add or rename columns directly; in company‑managed spaces you adjust them in board settings【765263728887536†L302-L329】.
  4. Create a work item – Work items (stories, epics, bugs, etc.) represent tasks. Click Create to add items to your backlog or board【765263728887536†L335-L343】.
  5. Connect your tools – Jira integrates with thousands of apps. Go to Apps → Find new apps to install integrations like GitHub, Slack or CI/CD tools【765263728887536†L348-L359】.
  6. Invite your team – Once your board contains work items, invite team members so they can collaborate【765263728887536†L360-L365】.
  7. Move work forward – In a Scrum project, start a sprint; in a Kanban project, begin moving items across columns【765263728887536†L369-L375】.

Tips for beginners

  • Use templates – Scrum templates support time‑boxed sprints; Kanban templates support continuous flow; bug‑tracking templates provide a simple list view【765263728887536†L247-L276】.
  • Team‑managed vs. company‑managed – Team‑managed spaces let individual teams control settings, while company‑managed spaces are centrally administered and good for standardized workflows【765263728887536†L286-L296】.
  • Integrations – Connect Jira to GitHub or Slack to automate tasks (e.g., linking pull requests to issues or sending notifications)【765263728887536†L348-L359】.
